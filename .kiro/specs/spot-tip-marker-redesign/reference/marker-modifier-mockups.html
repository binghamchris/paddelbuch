Revised halo — open at the bottom so it clears the neck

Instead of a closed ring (which crosses the neck/tail), the halo becomes an open horseshoe hugging the head from shoulder to shoulder over the top. The opening is anchored to the exact points where the pin's neck meets the head, so the tail stays completely clear. Beads sit on the arc, split-coloured for two tips, and are pushed out to ~r32 so they clear the marker's own content (e.g. the entry/exit arrowhead).

The problem with the current approach

Two spots close together. With detached corner discs it gets ambiguous which badge belongs to which pin, and the discs nibble the coloured head.

The four options in detail

Option 1 · recommended

Docked tab on the neck

1 tip
2 tips
on a rest pin

A rounded tab fused to the pin over the empty neck, holding the tip glyphs in white wells.

Pros
  • Part of one silhouette — unambiguous ownership
  • Covers only dead space; head & glyph fully visible
  • Compact width — clusters cleanly
Cons
  • Tight for 3+ tips
  • Adds a little height below the head
Option 2

Pennant flags on a mast

1 tip
2 tips

A short mast off the head flies one colour-coded pennant per tip.

Pros
  • Eye-catching; reads as "this pin's flag"
  • Sits outside the head — nothing covered
  • Stacks easily for more tips
Cons
  • Taller/wider footprint — more collisions when clustered
  • Busiest silhouette of the four
Option 3 · revised

Tip-coloured halo + beads

1 tip
2 tips
on a rest pin

An open horseshoe encircles the head (split-coloured for multiple tips) with a bead per tip seated on it. Open at the bottom so the neck stays clear; beads pushed out to clear the head glyph.

Pros
  • Ring ties the beads to the pin
  • Halo doubles as a highlight
  • Neck & glyph stay clear
Cons
  • Ring colour can clash with the head colour
  • Beads sit slightly proud of the open arc
Option 4

Rim studs / crown

1 tip
2 tips

Tip glyphs seated into the head's top edge like studs on a crown.

Pros
  • Tightly attached to the head outline
  • Smallest overall footprint
Cons
  • Subtle / easy to miss at map scale
  • Sits on the head rim — pushed inward it risks crowding the glyph
